What is Identity Theft?

Identity Theft is a very serious crime in which a criminal uses another person's personal information. A criminal can use their name, banking details and other details, typically for financial gain.

How to prevent Identity Theft?

There are many steps you can take to prevent Identity Theft. These include:

  1. Securely store documents, and shred them if needed, physically and digitally.
  2. Use strong passwords, and be sure not to reuse them.
  3. Monitor and check financial and banking accounts for anything suspicious.
  4. Protect your devices, being sure to check the articles in 'Learn about Device Security'.

Consequences of Identity Theft

Identity Theft can have many consequences physically, mentally and financially. Also, this can cause legal issues. Some examples of typical consequences include:

  1. Financial and credit-related issues.
  2. Potential legal issues.
  3. Mental health, emotional distress and reputational damage.

Real-life Example: Guarding Against Identity Theft

Let's explore a scenario involving a pervasive online threat known as Identity Theft.

Imagine you receive an email seemingly from your bank, alerting you to suspicious activity in your account that requires urgent attention. As you inspect the email, notice that it:

  • Displays the bank's logo, creating a false sense of legitimacy.
  • Addresses you by your full name, adding a personal touch.
  • Lacks spelling or grammatical errors, enhancing its credibility.

Trusting the email's authenticity, you click on the provided link and land on a website that mirrors your bank's official site. However, this is an Identity Theft attempt. Upon entering your details, the criminal gains access to your personal information for malicious purposes.

How did they trick you into clicking on this email?

Cybercriminals employ cunning tactics to make their emails appear genuine:

  1. The email seems legitimate, addressing you by your full name and featuring the bank's logo.
  2. The email creates a sense of urgency, claiming immediate attention is needed for your account.
  3. Clicking on the link takes you to a website that convincingly mimics your bank's official site.
  4. Unbeknownst to you, entering your details provides the criminal with sensitive information.

You suspect you've fallen victim to an Identity Theft attempt. What should you do?

Stay calm and take these steps to address the situation:

  1. Change any passwords you may have disclosed to the scammer.
  2. Contact your bank using a trusted method to report the incident and seek guidance.
  3. Don't be ashamed to share your experience with someone you trust and seek assistance if needed.
  4. Keep a close eye on your accounts for any suspicious activity and promptly report it.

How could we have avoided this?

Before proceeding, it is always best to:

  • Verify an email is from a legitimate source.

    • Banking Institutions, and other companies, will email from official email domains. If the email address looks fake, it might be! Contact the bank or company, or seek help, if needed.

  • Look for 'Red Flags'

    • Check for any spelling or grammar mistakes. Also, check for any messages which pressure you into doing something quickly or urgently.

  • Hover your mouse / cursor over a link before clicking on it

    • Hovering over a link will allow you to see where it goes. If this does not look like an official website, contact the company for help and/or open the website using a trusted method.
