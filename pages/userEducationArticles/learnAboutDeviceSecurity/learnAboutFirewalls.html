What is a Firewall?

Cyber threats are increasingly prevalent, causing financial, emotional, and physical harm. To counter these risks, companies have developed tools to prevent and mitigate these threats. One such tool is a Firewall, designed to prevent unauthorized access and protect your network from potential security breaches.

Why is a Firewall important?

The primary purpose of a firewall is to secure your network and data from cyber threats. Different firewall providers employ various methods, but many offerings typically include:

  • Prevention of unauthorized access.
  • Protection against network-based attacks.
  • Monitoring and controlling incoming and outgoing traffic.
  • Safeguarding sensitive data from unauthorized access.
  • Improved overall network security.

What does a Firewall protect against?

A Firewall is a crucial component of cybersecurity designed to perform several key functions. Its primary roles include:

  • Preventing unauthorized access to your network.
  • Protecting your system and data from network-based attacks.
  • Monitoring and controlling incoming and outgoing traffic.
  • Safeguarding sensitive data from unauthorized access.
  • Improving overall network security by acting as a barrier against potential threats.
